Bitcoin Gold Ratio

The Bitcoin Gold Ratio is a simple way to compare Bitcoin against gold. Instead of looking only at the dollar price of BTC, the ratio asks how Bitcoin is performing relative to a traditional store-of-value asset.

Bitcoin Gold Ratio = Bitcoin price / Gold price

What the ratio can show

When the ratio rises, Bitcoin is gaining value relative to gold. When the ratio falls, Bitcoin is weakening relative to gold. This can help users study broad market phases, but it should not be treated as a short-term trading signal by itself.

Why relative valuation matters

Dollar prices can be affected by inflation, liquidity, interest rates, and market cycles. A ratio can reduce some of that noise by comparing two scarce assets directly. BTC2G uses this idea to make long-term BTC vs Gold trends easier to see.

Limits of the ratio

No single ratio explains the full market. Bitcoin and gold have different risk profiles, investor bases, trading behavior, and macro sensitivity. The ratio is best used as one research input among many.
